Joseph K. Afrifa

Director
Ecologist
Ornithology, Site-Based Conservation, Urban Wildlife

An ecologist specialising in ornithology, site-based conservation, and species ecology. Currently a Research Fellow at KNUST Kumasi, he previously served at the Ghana Wildlife Society and BirdLife International Africa. His work spans ecosystem services, climate change, urban wildlife ecology, and human–wildlife conflict, with a strong focus on how environmental change and human activity shape wildlife systems across West Africa. With extensive experience in West African ornithology, he has developed a particular interest in the ecology and conservation of threatened birds, long-term bird monitoring, camera trap deployment and analysis, and the design of community-based conservation initiatives. As Director at WingWatch Africa, he brings both scientific depth and applied experience, supporting the organisation's efforts to link research, monitoring, and conservation action.

Abdulai Sulemana

Director
Wildlife Ecologist
Avian Ecology, Agroforestry, Ecosystem Resilience

A wildlife ecologist whose work investigates how habitat types, land-use practices, and human activities affect avian community composition and ecosystem functioning. He holds an MPhil in Wildlife Management from the University of Cape Coast, Ghana, and an MSc in Conservation Biology from the A.P. Leventis Ornithological Research Institute at the University of Jos, Nigeria. His recent work supports the development of policy and training for farmers to promote sustainable agriculture and biodiversity-friendly livelihoods. His publications focus on avian species and functional diversity in savannah ecosystems. As Director of WingWatch Africa, he provides strategic leadership with a focus on aligning research, policy, and implementation — emphasising a grounded form of conservation that acknowledges the realities of human-dominated landscapes while working to maintain the ecological functions that sustain them.

Samuel E. Tamekloe

Secretary & Communications Lead
Conservation Biologist
Functional Diversity, Wildlife Health, Science Communication

A conservation biologist interested in a simple but often overlooked idea: ecosystems can look intact and still be functionally broken. His work combines field research and statistical modelling to understand how human pressure reshapes animal communities — not just in terms of species loss, but in the loss of ecological roles. Focusing on birds across West African coastal and forest systems, he studies how urbanisation and habitat change filter communities toward a narrower set of generalist species, leaving ecosystems that are present but simplified. He has also worked at the intersection of wildlife health and conservation, exploring parasite transmission at the human–wildlife interface. As Secretary and Communications Lead at WingWatch Africa, his work focuses on translating ecological patterns into clear, honest narratives that challenge the idea that presence equals health.

Joseph K. Daniels

Founder & Research Lead
Ornithologist
Vulture Ecology, Urban Biodiversity

An ornithologist and conservation biologist dedicated to the study and protection of African bird species, with a particular focus on threatened vulture populations. His work spans field ecology, spatial analysis, and community-based conservation, grounded in extensive field experience across West Africa. He has contributed to vulture monitoring programs, urban biodiversity assessments, and research examining wildlife trade dynamics within traditional medicine markets. Based in Accra, he is particularly interested in species that persist within human-dominated landscapes and the ecological and social factors that determine their survival. As Founder and Research Lead of WingWatch Africa, he focuses on advancing science-driven conservation policy while strengthening local capacity for avian research and protection across the continent.
